George Washington

George Washington was the very first President of the United States. Before that, he was a general who helped lead America to independence from the British.

Early Life

Washington grew up in Virginia on a farm. As a teenager, he worked as a land surveyor. Early in his life he lead the Virginia militia and fought in the French and Indian War. These early experiences gave him leadership skills that he would use later in life.

Accomplishments

Washington is best known for leading the American army to victory in the Revolutionary War. After that, he became the first U.S. President and set examples that future presidents would follow.
